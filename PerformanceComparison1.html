Choose Rockwool if you need:

  1. Superior fire resistance (fire-rated walls, wildfire zones).
  2. Better thermal and sound insulation (energy savings & noise control).
  3. Moisture-resistant insulation (humid climates, basements, crawl spaces).
  4. Long-lasting, durable performance (50+ years without sagging).

Choose Fiberglass if you need:

  • A budget-friendly option (cheaper than Rockwool).
  • Lighter-weight insulation (easier to handle for large DIY projects).
  • Lower production emissions (though it may not last as long).

 

Overall Winner: Rockwool for Performance, Safety, and Longevity

While fiberglass is cheaper, Rockwool outperforms it in fire resistance, soundproofing, moisture protection, and durability, making it a better long-term investment.
